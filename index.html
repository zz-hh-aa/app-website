Hi, I'm Ollie

I currently work for a built environment non-profit, and before that I worked in music. Originally, I did a BA in fine art and art history. It might seem like an odd trajectory, but there's a lot of interesting art out there to do with digital tech and the internet, and that's first what got me interested in the field.

Music is still a big part of my life, but working in it wasn’t for me! It’s still something I enjoy outside of work, along with reading speculative fiction, growing things / volunteering on an agro-ecological farm, and learning about degrowth and sustainability.

My pronouns are they / them.

I’ve worked in the non-profit tech sector for a few years and while I love the field, I've always been frustrated that I can't build things myself. I've mostly done content and marketing (which I also can’t say is something I’m hugely passionate about), but I feel like career progression isn't possible without technical skills. While I’m fairly new to it, code is exciting; there’s so much more you can do with it and so many places you can go.

I feel like I have a decent grasp of certain technical concepts (from decentralisation to APIs), and while I've followed ethical tech projects and learned a bit about how they worked conceptually, I could never get my hands dirty and contribute. Projects I'm thinking of here include the (now-defunct) co-op broadcaster db, alternative social media platforms scuttlebutt and are.na, and low-tech websites, I could never get my hands dirty and get involved or contribute.

Furthermore, since tech is the main way many of us interface with the outside world, I think it’s one of those skills that everyone should have! I think basic literacy in the things we use every day is important.

The non-profit, social enterprise tech sector is someting I'm passionate about. Outside of work, I’ve been involved in various mutual aid, organising, or volunteering projects and it’s been a long-time dream of mine to build or contribute to tools for social movements. (One idea I’ve had in my head for ages is a ‘gender atlas’ type thing, inspired by the Atlas of Ownership I got to contribute to through work.)

Long story short, building stuff is exciting and I want to be able to do it!

FAC’s values-led approach really resonates with me, and it's the only socially-minded bootcamp I've been able to find. Because of shared values, I think I’d be a good fit in the course culture. Beyond that, as a gender non-conforming person, I feel that it would be the most inclusive and welcoming place for me to learn.
